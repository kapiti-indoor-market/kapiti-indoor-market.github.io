KAPITI INDOOR MARKET

Kapiti Indoor Market is a new market coming to the Kapiti Coast.

Kapiti Indoor Market is a new market showcasing only the best products from a select range of local businesses, artisans, crafters and food trucks.

The Market creates an enjoyable shopping experience for customers who want to browse a vast array of products & services all under one roof. 

Kapiti Indoor Market is a not-for-profit organisation. Each market event will host a charity in raising funds for their cause.
The Market supports small businesses who might not have their own premises. Kapiti Indoor Market gives them a chance to get their products & services out there.

Kapiti Indoor Market will be held quarterly, with the first market taking place on Sunday 24th June 2018.

The market will take place in Raumati Beach, the the large sports hall at Kapiti College. See below for directions.

Visit the market

The market is held in the sports hall at Kapiti College in Raumati Beach.
Parking: there are two large car parks at Kapiti College, one accessible from Raumati Road and the other from Hillcrest Road. There is also a large car park in Raumati Beach Village, along with parking on most of the surrounding streets.

Local services: There is an ATM in the village next to the Four Square. 
​
Other attractions: Raumati Village is a thriving hub with plenty of things to see & explore. Try the Miniature railway or the fab playground in Marine Gardens.

Market aims

Kapiti Indoor Market was set up to:

  • Help local artists, designers and small businesses to thrive
  • Create a tangible experience of the ‘Creative Kapiti’
  • Be an example of a sustainable business.

The journey so far

Kapiti Indoor Market was set up by two locals who each run a business and were looking for an affordable way to showcase their products without getting blown away at the outside markets.
Rachel Killen lives in the heart of the Kapiti Coast in sunny Raumati Beach.  She is the Business Change Manager for an investment company in the city and has experience in managing projects of all sizes.
​
As the owner of a small hand-crafted business, Wee Squiddlies, Rachel is passionate about providing an event where small artisan businesses can showcase their products.  She is also passionate about staying out of the weather!
Nicola van Rooijen also lives in Raumati Beach. She currently runs two businesses from home, after having quit her project management job in the city following the birth of her second child.

Nicola has an enthusiasm for sustainability, and both of her businesses reflect this. Charlie & Flo's gives pre-loved clothing another adventure, and Just Bring Baby aims to make your travels easier by renting what you need rather than buying it. She likes the idea of a market allowing customers to get up close & personal with products from small businesses that don't have their own premises.
